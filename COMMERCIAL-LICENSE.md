**Commercial use is not covered by the noncommercial license and requires a
separate commercial license** from the copyright holder.

## What counts as commercial use?

Examples of use that require a commercial license include (but are not limited to):

- Use by a for-profit company in its products, services, or internal R&D
- Use to provide a paid product or service (including SaaS) to third parties
- Use in commercially funded drug discovery, target identification, biomarker
  development, or diagnostics
- Redistribution as part of a commercial offering

If you are unsure whether your intended use is commercial, please ask.

## Intellectual property

The PAR(2) methodology (including the AR(2) eigenvalue-modulus persistence metric
and phase-gating analysis) is the subject of a **pending patent application in the
United Kingdom**. The patent covers the underlying methodology and analytical
methods independently of the software license. A commercial license can be
structured to cover both the software and, where applicable, rights to practise
the patented method.
